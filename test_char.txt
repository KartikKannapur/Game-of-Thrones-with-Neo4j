<p>Prince <b>Joffrey Baratheon</b> is known to the <a href="/index.php/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> as the eldest son and heir of King <a href="/index.php/Robert_I_Baratheon" title="Robert I Baratheon">Robert I Baratheon</a> and Queen <a href="/index.php/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a>. A member of <a href="/index.php/House_Baratheon_of_King%27s_Landing" title="House Baratheon of King's Landing">House Baratheon of King's Landing</a>, his siblings are Princess <a href="/index.php/Myrcella_Baratheon" title="Myrcella Baratheon">Myrcella</a> and Prince <a href="/index.php/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen</a>. In the television adaptation <i><a href="/index.php/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i>, he is played by Jack Gleeson.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance_and_Character">Appearance and Character</span></h2>
<dl><dd><i>See also: <a href="/index.php/Category:Images_of_Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Category:Images of Joffrey Baratheon">Images of Joffrey Baratheon</a></i></dd></dl>
<p>Twelve years old at the beginning of <i><a href="/index.php/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Joffrey was strong-willed as a child and has an uncontrollable temper not unlike his mother's and an unchecked sadistic streak. He has little sense of right or wrong, which often leads him to trouble, especially when he loses his temper. When things go wrong, he always blames the problems on others. Despite being willful he is reckless, vicious, cruel and not very intelligent, all of which combine to make him prone to irrational and bad judgements.
</p><p>Joffrey has the <a href="/index.php/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannister</a> look and is tall for a boy his age, with blond curly hair. Known to be handsome, he has bright green eyes, pouty lips and an evil sneer. <a href="/index.php/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> thought that Joffrey looks like a girl. He always wears the finest clothing and accents, as befits the royal heir.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot5.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-4">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot7.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-5">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>Joffrey grew up as a spoiled and indulgent child with a cruel streak within him. His father, King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/index.php/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert</a>, is deeply disappointed with his son and feels little affection for Joffrey. It is also implied that Joffrey craves Robert's respect and approval, and many of his acts are done in an effort to live up to his father's standards.
</p><p>Once after learning a kitchen cat was pregnant, Joffrey killed the animal and cut open its belly to see the kittens inside. He showed one of the unborn kittens to his father. Robert was so shocked and angry he hit the boy so hard it knocked out two of his baby teeth. Cersei also remarks that Robert would have beaten the boy if she had allowed it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rasos63.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-7">[8]</a></sup> It is also implied Joffrey bullied his younger brother, <a href="/index.php/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Raffc8.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-8">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>For Joffrey's 12th nameday, King Robert organizes a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/index.php/Tourney_for_Prince_Joffrey%27s_12th_Name_Day" title="Tourney for Prince Joffrey's 12th Name Day">tourney</a>, where, amongst others, Lord <a href="/index.php/Walder_Frey" title="Walder Frey">Walder Frey</a>, Lord <a href="/index.php/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>, Lord <a href="/index.php/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a> and Ser <a href="/index.php/Davos_Seaworth" title="Davos Seaworth">Davos Seaworth</a> are present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot59.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-9">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Racok0.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-10">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-11">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_Events">Recent Events</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A_Game_of_Thrones"><i>A Game of Thrones</i></span></h3>
<p>Joffrey and the rest of the royal family take the <a href="/index.php/Kingsroad" title="Kingsroad">kingsroad</a> to <a href="/index.php/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> after <a href="/index.php/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>'s death. There he begins to court the pretty <a href="/index.php/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a>, <a href="/index.php/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard</a>'s daughter, to whom he is betrothed. He spars with <a href="/index.php/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> in the yard and insults him. After <a href="/index.php/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a> is critically injured after falling from a tower, Joffrey's uncle, <a href="/index.php/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a>, slaps Joffrey for speaking rudely of the boy's condition. Tyrion is later blamed when an assassin with a <a href="/index.php/Valyrian_steel" title="Valyrian steel">Valyrian dagger</a> fails in an attempt to murder the comatose Bran, which furthers the growing enmity between Houses <a href="/index.php/House_Stark" title="House Stark">Stark</a> and <a href="/index.php/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannister</a>.
</p><p>On the way from Winterfell to <a href="/index.php/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, Joffrey spends a day riding with Sansa, who enjoys it greatly whilst he drinks summerwine. They come upon <a href="/index.php/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> and her friend <a href="/index.php/Mycah" title="Mycah">Mycah</a>, the butcher's boy, practicing sword fighting. Joffrey is drunk and commands Mycah to spar with him; Mycah does not take up the challenge, and Joffrey pricks him with his sword, <a href="/index.php/Lion%27s_Tooth" title="Lion's Tooth">Lion's Tooth</a>, ignoring pleas to leave him alone. Arya smacks him with the stick, allowing Mycah to escape; an enraged Joffrey slashes at Arya and is injured when her direwolf, <a href="/index.php/Nymeria_(direwolf)" title="Nymeria (direwolf)">Nymeria</a>, protects her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot15.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-12">[13]</a></sup> It is at this time that Joffrey loses Lion's Tooth, as Arya takes it from him and sends it hurling towards <a class="mw-redirect" href="/index.php/The_Trident" title="The Trident">the Trident</a>, an incident that humiliates Joffrey. The youth later claims before Robert's court that he had been attacked by Mycah and Nymeria. This results in the deaths of Mycah and Sansa's direwolf, <a href="/index.php/Lady_(direwolf)" title="Lady (direwolf)">Lady</a>, and earns Joffrey Arya's hatred. <a href="/index.php/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a> openly laughs at his nephew for being beaten and disarmed by a girl younger than him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot16.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-13">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>While drinking with Eddard Stark at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/index.php/Tourney_of_the_Hand" title="Tourney of the Hand">Tourney of the Hand</a>, King Robert openly despairs of his son and heir, revealing to his old friend that he has often thought of abdicating; the only thing that stops him is the thought of Joffrey sitting on the Iron Throne with his mother whispering in his ear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot30.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-14">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>Acting as the new <a href="/index.php/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>, Eddard Stark discovers that Joffrey, along with his siblings, are actually bastards born of incest between Cersei and her twin, <a href="/index.php/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot44.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-15">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot45.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-16">[17]</a></sup> Robert never questioned the boy's parentage. Cersei protects her children's secret by arranging for King Robert's death. Following Robert's death, Joffery summons the council and commands that they make arrangements for his coronation. When Ned produces Robert's will, which declares Ned regent and mentions "the heir" rather than specifying Joffrey as heir to the <a href="/index.php/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>, Cersei tears it up and advises Ned to swear fealty to her son. Ned in turn reveals that Joffrey has no claim to the throne and that his uncle, <a href="/index.php/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>, is the true heir, but Eddard is quickly arrested for treason.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot49.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-17">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a class="image" href="/index.php/File:MagaliVilleneuve_JoffLannister.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="266" src="/images/thumb/5/54/MagaliVilleneuve_JoffLannister.jpg/350px-MagaliVilleneuve_JoffLannister.jpg" srcset="/images/thumb/5/54/MagaliVilleneuve_JoffLannister.jpg/525px-MagaliVilleneuve_JoffLannister.jpg 1.5x, /images/5/54/MagaliVilleneuve_JoffLannister.jpg 2x" width="350"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/index.php/File:MagaliVilleneuve_JoffLannister.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Joffrey sitting on the <a href="/index.php/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> - by Magali Villeneuve ©</div></div></div>
<p>After taking the Iron Throne as Robert's heir, Joffrey starts his first court session by naming <a href="/index.php/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> as the new Hand of the King, appointing his mother to the <a href="/index.php/Small_council" title="Small council">small council</a> and <a href="/index.php/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> as <a href="/index.php/Lord_Commander_of_the_Kingsguard" title="Lord Commander of the Kingsguard">Lord Commander</a> of the <a href="/index.php/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>, dismissing the legendary knight <a href="/index.php/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> from his service, against all traditions. When Selmy storms out in disgust, making a remark about the ease with which Stannis would take the throne from him, Joffrey orders Selmy seized and questioned, though the old knight escapes.
</p><p>When Sansa kneels and begs for him to spare her father Eddard's life and asks that Joffrey to do this for love of her, Joffrey promises Sansa that he would be merciful. After Eddard confesses of his crimes and declares that Joffrey Baratheon is the true heir to the Iron Throne, Joffrey's mercy turns out to be Eddard's quick public beheading before Sansa's eyes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot65.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-18">[19]</a></sup> This rash act is against his family's wishes to minimize further bloodshed and restore the king's peace and leads to the intensification of <a href="/index.php/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">hostilities</a> as Eddard's son <a href="/index.php/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> is declared the <a href="/index.php/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King in the North</a>. Robb vows to kill Joffrey in retaliation, plunging Westeros into devastating civil war.
</p><p>The new king fancies himself a warrior, and considers leading the <a href="/index.php/City_Watch_of_King%27s_Landing" title="City Watch of King's Landing">City Watch of King's Landing</a> into the field. Cersei withholds the news of Lord Renly's coronation from him lest he insist on marching against him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot69.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-19">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>Joffrey continues to mistreat and abuse Sansa Stark, a penalty for each of Robb's victories. Forcing her to look at her father's severed head is the start of a string of beatings he commands his Kingsguard to abuse her with. He threatens to force her to look on <a href="/index.php/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">her brother</a>'s head when he kills him, as he boasts, in single combat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragot67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-20">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A_Clash_of_Kings"><i>A Clash of Kings</i></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/index.php/File:MagaliVilleneuve_JoffreyBaratheon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="333" src="/images/thumb/0/08/MagaliVilleneuve_JoffreyBaratheon.jpg/400px-MagaliVilleneuve_JoffreyBaratheon.jpg" srcset="/images/0/08/MagaliVilleneuve_JoffreyBaratheon.jpg 1.5x, /images/0/08/MagaliVilleneuve_JoffreyBaratheon.jpg 2x" width="400"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/index.php/File:MagaliVilleneuve_JoffreyBaratheon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>King Joffrey Baratheon - by Magali Villeneuve ©</div></div></div>
<p>Joffrey rules with whim and caprice, proving difficult for even his mother to control. Sansa becomes imprisoned to his will, and he frequently has his guards beat her when she displeases him. When he tries to have her stripped, his uncle, <a href="/index.php/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a>, stops him. Although Joffrey never tries it again, he is determined to eventually have Sansa in his bed whether he marries her or not. His cruelty and the decreased quality of life at King's Landing due to food shortages and other hardships make Joffrey an unpopular king, and he is nearly killed in a <a href="/index.php/Riot_of_King%27s_Landing" title="Riot of King's Landing">riot</a> sparked by his temper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Racok41.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-21">[22]</a></sup> Acting as his temporary Hand, only Tyrion stands up to Joffrey's authority, and the king develops a special hatred for his uncle; Tyrion, in return, holds his nephew in contempt, viewing him as a monster.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A_Storm_of_Swords"><i>A Storm of Swords</i></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/index.php/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a> allies itself to his reign, Joffrey discards Sansa and is betrothed to <a href="/index.php/Margaery_Tyrell" title="Margaery Tyrell">Margaery Tyrell</a>. He crassly declares that he will have his way with Sansa later, after the wedding, even though she is married to Tyrion. Joffrey is present with <a href="/index.php/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a>, <a href="/index.php/Kevan_Lannister" title="Kevan Lannister">Kevan Lannister</a>, <a href="/index.php/Pycelle" title="Pycelle">Pycelle</a>, <a href="/index.php/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> and <a href="/index.php/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin</a> when news reaches Kings Landing of the <a href="/index.php/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>. Joffrey is delighted when he hears of <a href="/index.php/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s death and wants his head so he could serve it to Sansa at his own wedding, in addition to wanting to show no mercy to the northern and river lords who surrendered following Robb Stark's death. This statement horrifies Kevan and enrages Tyrion, who threatens his nephew, stating that Sansa is not his to torment. Tywin refuses to allow Joffrey's request, which causes Joffrey to insult his grandfather as "a coward who hid beneath Casterly Rock" while his father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/index.php/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert</a> won the war at <a href="/index.php/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">the Trident</a>. The insult angers Tywin to the point that in private he remarks that Joffrey requires a sharp lesson. Tyrion, despite remembering his own "<a href="/index.php/Tysha" title="Tysha">sharp lesson</a>", surprisingly agrees with this assessment, though he insists that his nephew is not Robert the Second but Aerys the Third.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rasos53.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-22">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a class="image" href="/index.php/File:The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="246" src="/images/thumb/3/3b/The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG/350px-The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG" srcset="/images/thumb/3/3b/The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG/525px-The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG 1.5x, /images/thumb/3/3b/The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG/700px-The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG 2x" width="350"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/index.php/File:The_Purple_Wedding_Fin.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Depiction of The Purple Wedding by <a class="external text" href="https://www.facebook.com/ConorCampbellArtist?ref=hl/" rel="nofollow">Conor Campbell©</a></div></div></div>
<p>Joffrey's marriage is on the first day of the new century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rasos4.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-2">[3]</a></sup> In the morning the Lannisters and their allies meet for a family breakfast. His grandfather, <a href="/index.php/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a>, gives him a <a href="/index.php/Valyrian_steel" title="Valyrian steel">Valyrian steel</a> sword as a wedding present, which Joffrey declares he will name <a href="/index.php/Widow%27s_Wail" title="Widow's Wail">Widow's Wail</a>. The present from Tyrion and Sansa is a rare book, <i><a href="/index.php/Lives_of_Four_Kings" title="Lives of Four Kings">Lives of Four Kings</a></i>, which Joff slices with his new sword, demanding a better present from Tyrion and Sansa. Joffrey swings the blade at <a href="/index.php/Balon_Swann" title="Balon Swann">Balon Swann</a>. When <a href="/index.php/Addam_Marbrand" title="Addam Marbrand">Addam Marbrand</a> warns Joffrey to be careful, since the blade is Valyrian steel, Joffrey boasts that he is no stranger to the metal. At that moment, Tyrion baits him, guessing that Joffrey is referring to the Valyrian dagger given to the assassin at Winterfell, though he is unable to fathom why, attributing it to Joffrey's innate cruelty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-3">[4]</a></sup> Jaime later deduces that Joffrey did it in an attempt to impress his father after overhearing a drunken Robert say it would be kinder to put the crippled <a href="/index.php/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a> out of his misery.
</p><p>During his wedding feast in the throne room, Joffrey's wine is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/index.php/The_Strangler" title="The Strangler">poisoned</a> and he dies on the first day of <a href="/index.php/Years_after_Aegon%27s_Conquest#Year_300_After_the_Conquest" title="Years after Aegon's Conquest">300 AC</a>, while the entire court looks on, his face turning black as he asphyxiates while tearing at his throat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rasos60.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-3">[4]</a></sup> Cersei blames the deed on Tyrion and Sansa, but it is later revealed that the plot was orchestrated by <a href="/index.php/Olenna_Redwyne" title="Olenna Redwyne">Olenna Redwyne</a> and <a href="/index.php/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a>. Joffrey is laid in state in the castle sept in gilded armor, and when Jaime returns to King's Landing he makes love to Cersei in front of the corpse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rasos62.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-23">[24]</a></sup> He is ultimately laid to rest in the <a href="/index.php/Great_Sept_of_Baelor" title="Great Sept of Baelor">Great Sept of Baelor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rasos67.7B.7B.7B3.7D.7D.7D-24">[25]</a></sup> Due to the color of the amethysts used to poison Joffrey's wine, and the actual color of the wine, the wedding has since been referred to by fans as the <a href="/index.php/Purple_Wedding" title="Purple Wedding">Purple Wedding</a>.
</p><p>Joffrey, with the exception of his <a href="/index.php/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">mother</a>, is not particularly mourned by anyone; even his biological father, <a href="/index.php/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a>, feels that Joffrey deserved his fate. Tyrion Lannister states that Joffrey would have become a worse king than <a href="/index.php/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">the Mad King</a>. <a href="/index.php/Arys_Oakheart" title="Arys Oakheart">Arys Oakheart</a> thinks the only good thing that could be said of Joffrey is that he was tall and strong for his age.
</p>
